Why Most Premium Estimates Are Wrong (And How GST Works)

Look, let's be brutally honest. Most online calculators fail because they use outdated GST rates. In India, life insurance premiums are taxed differently in the first year compared to subsequent years. If you don't calculate this properly, your monthly household budget will take a hit. Power cuts, rising school fees, and inflation in cities like Bhopal or Patna mean every rupee matters.

Calculation: Suppose your base premium is calculated at ₹30,000 yearly. The first-year GST rule applies 4.5%.
30,000 x 4.5% = ₹1,350.
Total you pay on day one = ₹31,350.
In the second year (and every year after), the GST drops to 2.25%.
30,000 x 2.25% = ₹675. Total you pay = ₹30,675. That gives you an extra ₹675 in savings every year from year two.

1. LIC Jeevan Labh (Plan 936)

This is the workhorse of middle-class savings. Imagine you are working a private job in a city where voltage spikes routinely fry your appliances and internet cuts are normal. You need financial stability. You invest in Jeevan Labh primarily because the premium paying term is significantly shorter than the policy term. You don't have to pay until the very end.

  • Power/Returns: typically yields around 6% to 6.5% IRR depending on the age of entry.
  • Eligibility: Minimum entry age is 8 years. Maximum age is 59 years.
  • Tax Benefit: Premiums are tax-free under Section 80C up to ₹1.5L. Maturity is fully exempt under Section 10(10D).

Watch out for: The heavy penalty if you surrender the policy before 3 full years of premium payments. You will lose massive amounts of your principal amount if you stop paying early.

Who is it for? Fathers planning a child's marriage in 21 years.

2. LIC New Jeevan Anand (Plan 915)

It is identical to a standard endowment plan, but with a major twist. You get your money when the policy matures. But the life insurance continues till you hit 100 years of age. Without paying another rupee.

  • Power/Returns: Normal maturity sum assured plus vested bonuses.
  • Lock-in: Must pay for at least 2 full years to acquire paid-up value.
  • Loan Facility: Yes, available after 2 years of making premium payments to manage sudden medical emergencies.

Watch out for: The premiums for Jeevan Anand are moderately higher than a standard endowment plan because LIC is carrying the risk of paying your nominee a post-maturity death benefit.

Who is it for? Breadwinners who want to leave a guaranteed estate to their children even after spending the maturity amount.

3. LIC New Tech Term (Plan 954)

This is a pure term insurance policy. You buy this purely online. There are no agents involved. It is essential if you have heavy liabilities like a home loan.

  • Coverage: Minimum Basic Sum Assured is ₹50 Lakhs.
  • Claim Settlement ratio: LIC famously boasts an incredibly high claim settlement ratio (historically over 98%).
  • Premiums: Incredibly low compared to endowments. A 30-year-old healthy male can get ₹1 Crore cover for barely ₹10,000 to ₹12,000 a year.

Watch out for: Zero maturity return. If you survive the policy term, you get ₹0 back. The money is purely the cost of coverage.

Who is it for? Anyone with financial dependents and a tight monthly budget who needs massive risk cover.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is LIC Jeevan Labh completely tax-free at maturity?

Yes. The maturity amount from Jeevan Labh is 100% tax-free under Income Tax Section 10(10D), provided the annual premium is less than 10% of the Basic Sum Assured.

Can I surrender my LIC policy after 1 year?

No. Most LIC endowment policies require you to pay premiums for at least 2 to 3 full years before a guaranteed surrender value is acquired. Stopping premiums earlier renders the policy lapsed.

What is the GST rate on LIC renewal premiums?

2.25%. While the first year attracts a 4.5% GST component, all subsequent renewal premiums for standard traditional policies are taxed exactly at 2.25%.
